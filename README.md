# Social Media Behaviour Analytics & Well-Being Modeling

End-to-end statistical analytics project examining the relationship between social media usage, mental health, sleep patterns, and campus engagement through survey design, data collection, regression modeling, hypothesis testing, and ANOVA.

## Project Overview

This study investigates how social media usage influences student well-being through:

- Mental health impact
- Sleep patterns
- Campus engagement
- Exposure to positive social media content

The project includes survey design, data collection, statistical analysis, and technical reporting.

## Dataset

The dataset consists of responses from 75 university students collected through a structured survey.

Sampling Method:
- Stratified Random Sampling
- 40 International Students
- 35 Domestic Students

## Methods

- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Summary Statistics
- Simple Linear Regression
- Multiple Linear Regression
- Comparison of Means (t-tests)
- ANOVA
- Data Visualization using ggplot2

## Key Findings

- Higher social media usage was associated with greater perceived impacts on sleep patterns and mental health.
- No statistically significant relationship was found between social media usage and campus engagement.
- Positive content exposure showed limited evidence of influencing perceived mental health outcomes.
